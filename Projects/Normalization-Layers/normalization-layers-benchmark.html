The Surprising Finding

The Identity (no-normalization) baseline wins on accuracy. BatchNorm hurts by 1.8 percentage points; LayerNorm by 0.9. The textbook story — "normalisation is essential for training deep networks" — is not what the data shows at this regime. Three reasons:

  • Tanh activations naturally bound activations to $[-1, 1]$.
  • AdamW is well-conditioned enough to compensate per-parameter.
  • BatchNorm's running statistics are noisy at small scale.

The Finding That Transfers

Among the normalisations that work, RMSNorm beats LayerNorm on every axis: fewer parameters, faster training, half the FLOPs, lower final loss. This is the Llama/Mistral/Gemma design choice in microcosm. The mean subtraction step in LayerNorm does no measurable work but costs a per-token reduce, a subtraction, and an additive bias parameter — real money at LLM scale.

Three Lessons

  1. Normalisation is not free magic. At small scale it can hurt as easily as it helps.
  2. Toy benchmarks lie about norm importance. Real production constraints make norms essential.
  3. The RMSNorm reframe travels: whenever a paper subtracts something, ask whether the subtraction is load-bearing.
