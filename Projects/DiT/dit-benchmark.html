Deconstructing DiT

Part 3: Training at Toy Scale, and Why It Matters

By Sovesh Mohapatra • May 2026
Overview

3,200 synthetic 16×16 colored-shape images. 16 classes (4 colors × 4 shapes). Class-conditional DiT, 323,696 params, 42 seconds CPU training.

Setup

Dataset. 16 classes at 16×16 RGB, 200 examples per class. Patch size 4 → 16 patches per image. Model. DiT, hidden=64, 4 layers, 4 heads, $323{,}696$ params. Diffusion. Linear $\beta$ schedule, $T = 200$. Training. AdamW lr=$5 \times 10^{-4}$, 30 epochs, batch 128. Hardware. CPU (MPS has a stride-handling bug in adaLN's backward; CUDA works fine).

Generated Samples

After training we generate one image per class from pure Gaussian noise with 200 ancestral denoising steps conditioned on the target class.

What is correct. Colors are recovered consistently for every class — class conditioning works.

What is approximate. Shape geometry is recognisable but blurry. Circles are roughly round but not smooth. Squares have soft edges. Crosses — the highest-frequency shape — are the hardest, often collapsing toward smudges with the right color.

Why the Samples Are Blurry

Three confounded reasons:

  1. Resolution is 16×16. A sharp triangle has a few pixels per edge.
  2. Dataset is tiny. 3,200 examples is enough to learn approximate marginals but not enough to learn sharp geometry from scratch with a low-inductive-bias architecture.
  3. Model is small. 323,696 parameters is two orders of magnitude smaller than the smallest published DiT.

All three point in the same direction: at this scale, the architecture's lack of inductive bias is a liability.

The Inductive-Bias Tradeoff, Quantified

U-Nets bake in image priors that you don't have to learn from data. DiTs learn whatever priors the data implies. At toy scale, baked-in priors win. At ImageNet scale, the tradeoff is roughly even. At LAION/JFT scale (hundreds of millions of images), DiT decisively wins — the U-Net's hardcoded biases become constraints. This is the regime that produced SD3 and Sora.

What This Series Demonstrates

The architecture is sound. Class conditioning works. Denoising trajectory has canonical DDPM appearance. What it cannot do at this scale is precisely the thing U-Nets do as a free gift: produce sharp output from a small corpus. The architecture is not the bottleneck. Scale is.
