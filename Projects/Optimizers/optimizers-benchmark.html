Deconstructing Optimizers

Part 3: Head-to-Head Benchmarks

By Sovesh Mohapatra • May 2026
Overview

Three problems. Per-optimizer learning rates. Identical initialisation. The winner depends entirely on the landscape.

Experiment 1 — Rosenbrock

OptimizerFinal $f(x, y)$$\|p - p^\star\|$
SGD$8.80 \times 10^{-3}$$0.20$
Momentum$\mathbf{6.26 \times 10^{-9}}$$\mathbf{1.8 \times 10^{-4}}$
Adam$4.02 \times 10^{-2}$$0.41$
AdamW$4.94 \times 10^{-2}$$0.45$
Lion$4.03$$2.00$

Momentum wins by six orders of magnitude. Rosenbrock has consistent gradient direction along the smooth valley floor; Momentum accumulates velocity and shrinks naturally as the gradient does. Adam's $\sqrt{\hat{v}}$ stabilises at a non-zero value near a smooth minimum, so Adam cannot land softly.

Experiment 2 — Beale

OptimizerFinal $f(x, y)$
Adam$\mathbf{2.89 \times 10^{-1}}$
AdamW$3.62 \times 10^{-1}$
Momentum$1.17$
SGD$1.40$
Lion$38.7$ (diverged)

The order reverses. Beale starts in a flat region with tiny gradients; Adam's $\sqrt{\hat{v}}$ normalisation inflates them to a useful magnitude. Adam wins by an order of magnitude.

Experiment 3 — MLP on Moons

OptimizerFinal lossFinal accuracy
SGD$0.2845$$88.30\%$
Momentum$0.2220$$91.20\%$
Adam$\mathbf{0.0746}$$\mathbf{97.70\%}$
AdamW$0.0759$$97.60\%$
Lion$0.0757$$97.50\%$

Adam, AdamW, Lion all cluster at $97.5$--$97.7\%$. Lion is competitive despite struggling on both 2D landscapes. On a real high-dimensional training problem, the differences between adaptive optimizers vanish into noise.

Practical Recipe

  • For a new model: AdamW, lr $3 \times 10^{-4}$ to $10^{-3}$, weight decay $0.01$, cosine schedule.
  • If optimizer-state memory matters: Lion at $\sim 1/10$ the AdamW lr.
  • Plain SGD is pedagogical; if you choose it, you should be able to articulate why.
